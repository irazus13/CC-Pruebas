## Resumen

**BACKGROUND:** Evans' Index (EI) is a widely used linear radiological measure of ventricular size on computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging. Despite the increasing use of volumetric neuroimaging techniques, EI remains a practical and commonly applied marker of ventricular enlargement in routine clinical practice. However, age- and sex-specific hospital-based reference values are limited in sub-Saharan Africa, where population-specific data are essential for accurate interpretation. This study aimed to establish age- and sex-specific hospital-based reference values for Evans' Index and cranial dimensions in a south-eastern Nigerian population using CT scans reported as normal despite clinical indications. **METHODS:** This retrospective cross-sectional study analysed 676 normal non-contrast cranial CT scans. Maximum anterior horn diameter (MAHD) and maximum internal cranial diameter (MICD) were measured at the level of the frontal horns, and EI was calculated as MAHD/MICD. Results from the non-normally distributed data were presented as median with quartile ranges (Q1-Q3) and percentile-based reference ranges (2.5th-97.5th percentiles). Associations with age and sex were assessed using non-parametric tests, and measurement reproducibility was evaluated using intraclass correlation coefficient analysis. **RESULTS:** Of the 676 participants, 268 (39.6%) were male and 408 (60.4%) were female. The overall median Evans' Index (EI) was 0.28 (Q1-Q3: 0.25-0.30), with a 95% reference range of 0.22-0.32. Females had significantly higher median EI (0.29 vs. 0.26) and larger MAHD compared with males, whereas males had significantly larger MICD (all p&#x2009;&lt;&#x2009;0.001, Mann-Whitney U test). EI demonstrated a strong positive correlation with age (Spearman's &#x3c1;&#x2009;=&#x2009;0.746; p&#x2009;&lt;&#x2009;0.001), and multivariable regression analysis confirmed age as an independent predictor of EI. Significant differences in EI, MAHD, and MICD were observed across age groups (Kruskal-Wallis p&#x2009;&lt;&#x2009;0.001 for all). Median EI increased progressively across age categories (0.24 in 1-18 years, 0.26 in 19-50 years, 0.29 in 51-80 years, and 0.30 in &#x2265;&#x2009;81 years), with corresponding increases in MAHD and a modest decline in MICD in older age groups. **CONCLUSIONS:** Evans' Index and cranial dimensions in south-eastern Nigeria vary significantly by age and sex. These hospital-based reference values are consistent with commonly used EI thresholds (&#x2265;&#x2009;0.30 for ventriculomegaly) while emphasising the need for age- and sex-stratified interpretation in clinical practice. These hospital-based reference values are derived from radiologically normal CT scans obtained during clinically indicated imaging and may not fully represent community-based healthy populations.
