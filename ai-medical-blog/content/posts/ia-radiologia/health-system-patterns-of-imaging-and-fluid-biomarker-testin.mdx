## Resumen

**INTRODUCTION:** Anti-amyloid-&#x3b2; (A&#x3b2;) therapies are reshaping Alzheimer's disease (AD) management. Understanding changes in real-world patterns of diagnostic testing and infusion chair usage is essential for optimizing access to care. **METHODS:** Retrospective analysis of Mayo Clinic enterprise electronic health records (Jan 2019-Mar 2025) assessed trends in AD-relevant brain imaging, fluid biomarkers, apolipoprotein E (APOE) testing, and lecanemab infusions. Rates of amyloid-beta (A&#x3b2;) positivity by sex and age, APOE genotype frequencies, and lecanemab treatment initiation and discontinuation were evaluated. **RESULTS:** Following national insurance coverage changes, lecanemab infusions grew by 110 infusions per quarter to 605 in Q1 2025. A&#x3b2; positron emission tomography scans increased (+22/quarter), cerebrospinal fluid biomarker orders declined (-25/quarter), and plasma p-tau217 orders rapidly increased (+238/quarter). Females were more likely to be A&#x3b2; positive (p&#xa0;&lt;&#xa0;0.006). APOE-&#x3b5;4 homozygotes were less likely to initiate lecanemab (HR&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.11, p&#xa0;&lt;&#xa0;0.001). **DISCUSSION:** The adoption of anti-A&#x3b2; therapies coincided with a rapid shift in diagnostic workflows.
