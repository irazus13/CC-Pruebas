## Resumen

We report a case of&#xa0;probable cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA) presenting with almost 100 cerebral microhemorrhages in&#xa0;an&#xa0;apolipoprotein E 4 (APOE4)&#xa0;homozygous subject with&#xa0;mild cognitive impairment (MCI).&#xa0;CAA is highly prevalent&#xa0;in autopsy studies&#xa0;and may impact&#xa0;over&#xa0;100 million individuals worldwide; however, it&#xa0;remains&#xa0;substantially underdiagnosed clinically. CAA raises the risk for intracerebral hemorrhages&#xa0;as well as amyloid-related imaging abnormalities (ARIA) with amyloid-targeted antibodies. CAA&#xa0;is especially a quandary in&#xa0;APOE4 homozygotes who are&#xa0;at&#xa0;greatest risk for both Alzheimer's disease (AD)&#xa0;and ARIA.&#xa0;This case&#xa0;highlights&#xa0;the&#xa0;urgent need for greater diagnostic awareness and therapeutic development.
