## Resumen

**BACKGROUND:** Artificial intelligence (AI) is becoming increasingly prevalent in the modern world, including in veterinary medicine. This cross-sectional study aimed to investigate horse carers' attitudes towards using AI use in equine care. **METHODS:** An online&#xa0;survey was distributed to UK horse owners/carers&#xa0;in 2025, covering&#xa0;participants'&#xa0;demographics and&#xa0;use of AI&#xa0;and&#xa0;their opinions&#xa0;of AI for equine care. Statistical analysis included&#xa0;descriptive statistics,&#xa0;categorisation of free-text responses and&#xa0;logistic regression&#xa0;to&#xa0;determine&#xa0;factors associated with opinions. **RESULTS:** Ninety-seven responses were analysed. Participants had a predominantly positive opinion of&#xa0;AI&#xa0;to&#xa0;automate&#xa0;large datasets for equine care, and a predominantly negative opinion for&#xa0;automating&#xa0;communications and medical decision making. Key categories identified in free-text responses&#xa0;were: AI use in general/equine care,&#xa0;desire for human interaction&#xa0;and&#xa0;AI&#xa0;as a supportive&#xa0;aid&#xa0;only.&#xa0;Positive attitudes towards AI for equine care were significantly associated with&#xa0;participants'&#xa0;opinions of AI in their own lives (odds ratio [OR]: 3.69, 95% confidence interval [CI]: 3.06&#x2012;4.45) and understanding of AI (OR: 1.31, 95% CI 1.03&#x2012;1.66). **LIMITATIONS:** This is a small&#xa0;exploratory&#xa0;study of horse owners/carers in the UK, and the findings may not be more widely generalisable. **CONCLUSION:** Horse&#xa0;owners/carers&#xa0;had mixed opinions on the use of AI in equine care, and their primary concern was around it replacing human decision making.
