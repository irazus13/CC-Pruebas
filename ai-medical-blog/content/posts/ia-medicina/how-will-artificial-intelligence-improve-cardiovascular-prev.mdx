## Resumen

Artificial intelligence (AI) is no longer just a passing trend. It is slowly becoming an integral part of our daily lives and our medical practice. AI opens new avenues for improving the cardiovascular prevention field. This article describes two viewpoints: a) how AI helps the patient to manage his/her cardiovascular risk factors and to educate himself/herself and b) how AI helps clinicians to improve their clinical practice. The day-to-day clinical practice already integrates smartwatches and large language models. Meanwhile, risk scores using machine learning and genetic data are still in the validation process.
