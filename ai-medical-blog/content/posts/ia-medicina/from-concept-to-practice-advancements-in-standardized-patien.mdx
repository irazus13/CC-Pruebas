## Resumen

The use of standardized patients (SPs) in medical education has evolved significantly over the past few decades, serving as a crucial component in the training and assessment of healthcare professionals. This narrative review explores recent innovations and developments in the field, focusing on enhancing realism, improving training protocols, and refining assessment techniques. As the demand for high-quality patient-centered care rises, so too does the need for more effective simulation-based education methods. It examines key advancements such as the integration of high-fidelity simulations, the use of virtual and hybrid models, and the incorporation of advanced feedback mechanisms. These innovations aim to improve learner outcomes by providing more realistic, nuanced patient interactions that better prepare healthcare professionals for real-world scenarios. Furthermore, we discuss the evolving role of technology, such as artificial intelligence (AI)-assisted simulations and virtual reality (VR), in transforming SP methodologies. It also addresses challenges, including ensuring standardization across various institutions, maintaining patient safety, and addressing the psychological impact on SPs. By bridging the gap between conceptual innovation and practical application, these advancements contribute to more effective training programs, ultimately enhancing the competence and confidence of future healthcare providers. Key areas for future research are also identified, particularly regarding the long-term impact of these methodologies on both patient outcomes and healthcare systems.
