## Resumen

Managing user consent for personal data remains a persistent challenge across domains such as healthcare, digital services, and research. Static consent mechanisms often fail to reflect user diversity, evolving preferences, and continuous regulatory changes. As part of the CONSENT project, this paper introduces a conceptual framework for Profile-aware Consent Management. The proposed framework leverages user profiling, adaptive form generation, and automated consenting agents to personalize and simplify the consent process while maintaining compliance and transparency. Building upon multi-stakeholder insights gathered from clinicians, citizens, IT managers, and GDPR experts, the study identifies the major pain points in current consent workflows and translates them into technical design requirements. The resulting framework envisions an AI-powered, profile-aware system that dynamically adapts consent presentation and decisions to user needs, supported by a regulatory compliance backbone.
