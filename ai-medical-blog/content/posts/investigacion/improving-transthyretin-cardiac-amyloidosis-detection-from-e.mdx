## Resumen

**BACKGROUND:** Transthyretin cardiac amyloidosis (ATTR-CM ) is a frequently underdiagnosed disease where delay in diagnosis limits the efficacy of treatments. Artificial Intelligence (AI) applied to standard 12-lead electrocardiograms (ECGs) is promising and can help improve ATTR-CM early recognition. **OBJECTIVE:** To extend the Willem AI cloud platform for ATTR-CM detection from 12-lead ECGs and evaluate its performance across relevant patient subgroups, including early-stage disease. **METHODS:** Single-center retrospective study of adults evaluated for ATTR-CM . Controls had negative DPD scintigraphy and clinical assessment excluding ATTR-CM . After quality control, 20,754 ECGs from 2,901 individuals (585 ATTR-CM , 2,316 controls) were used. Patients were split into training/validation and test sets with stratification by status, sex, and age. A time-slicing convolutional neural network generated ECG probabilities aggregated by max-pooling. Threshold selection targeted 90% sensitivity in validation. **RESULTS:** In the test cohort, Willem AI achieved an AUC of 0.88 (95% CI 0.85-0.91), sensitivity 80.7%, and specificity 78.5%. Performance was similar for ATTRv (AUC 0.91) and ATTRwt (AUC 0.88) and remained informative in early presentations (asymptomatic sensitivity 68.4%; NYHA class I 73.9%). Willem AI achieved high accuracy in "red-flag" scenarios: carpal tunnel syndrome (AUC 0.92), lumbar spinal stenosis (AUC 0.92), polyneuropathy (AUC 0.91). **CONCLUSION:** This work expands Willem AI capabilities, enabling automated ATTR-CM detection from routine 12-lead ECGs in a real-world referral population. Willem AI model may support informed clinical management and scalable triage to trigger confirmatory testing and accelerate diagnosis.
