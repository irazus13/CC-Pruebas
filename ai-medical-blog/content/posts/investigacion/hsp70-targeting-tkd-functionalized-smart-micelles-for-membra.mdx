## Resumen

Breast cancer remains the most frequently diagnosed malignancy among women worldwide, characterised by high recurrence, therapeutic resistance, and significant molecular heterogeneity.Membrane-bound heat shock protein 70 (mHSP70) has emerged as a tumour-selective biomarker, exhibiting elevated expression in aggressive breast cancer subtypes with minimal presence in normal tissues.Functionally, mHSP70 contributes to immune evasion, metastatic progression, and chemoresistance, making it a compelling therapeutic target. The 14-mer TKD peptide (TKDNNLGRFELSG) exhibits high-affinity binding to mHSP70, facilitating targeted drug delivery to HSP70-positive malignant cells.Preclinical investigations demonstrate that TKD peptide-functionalized nanocarriers enhance tumour penetration, reverse multidrug resistance, and elicit anti-tumour immune responses.However, successful clinical translation requires optimisation of pharmacokinetics, immunological compatibility, and in vivo stability. This review critically evaluates the biological role of mHSP70 in breast cancer, its diagnostic and therapeutic implications, and the translational potential of TKD-guided nanocarriers.The integration of precision nanotechnology with mHSP70-targeted therapy represents a potentially promising approach to overcoming treatment resistance and improving outcomes in metastatic breast cancer; however, further validation and translational research are required to confirm its clinical applicability.
