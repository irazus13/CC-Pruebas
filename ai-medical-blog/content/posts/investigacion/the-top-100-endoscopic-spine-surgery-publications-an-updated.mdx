## Resumen

**BACKGROUND:** To identify the current top 100 most-cited publications on endoscopic spine surgery (ESS) and analyze emerging trends from the past 5 years. **METHODS:** A 2-step process was used to identify the top 100 most-cited articles. Eight key phrases commonly used in ESS research were utilized to collect relevant articles from 3 research databases. The list from each database was arranged by number of citations and compared with determine the top 100 most-cited publications in ESS. **RESULTS:** The top 100 most-cited publications had a minimum of 94 citations and a maximum of 648. Most of the articles were published after 2006, and nearly one-third were published in Spine. South Korea, the United States, and Germany were the biggest contributors. **CONCLUSION:** During the past 5 years, research productivity has increased exponentially. The updated list demonstrates emerging trends within the field, including changing levels of engagement from different countries and authors, along with new techniques and pathologies. **CLINICAL RELEVANCE:** This analysis highlights the most influential research shaping current practices in ESS, offering clinicians insight into evolving surgical techniques, outcomes, and global contributions. Understanding these trends can guide evidence-based decisions and promote the adoption of innovative and minimally invasive approaches.
