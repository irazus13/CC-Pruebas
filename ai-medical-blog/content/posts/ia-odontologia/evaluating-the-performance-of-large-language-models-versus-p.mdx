## Resumen

**STATEMENT OF PROBLEM:** Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) offer expanded reasoning, multimodal processing, and improved factual grounding. However, their performance relative to prosthodontic residents remains unknown, and comparisons can inform whether current training fosters higher-order reasoning beyond the capabilities of artificial intelligence (AI). **PURPOSE:** This study compared the performance of 3 advanced LLMs, ChatGPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro, and Claude Sonnet 4, with prosthodontic residents on the 2024 and 2025 National Prosthodontic Resident Examinations (NPREs). **MATERIAL AND METHODS:** Three hundred multiple-choice questions (MCQs) were obtained from the 2024 and 2025 NPRE (150 items per year). Each MCQ was entered verbatim into the 3 LLMs using zero-shot prompting in isolated sessions. Responses were scored against the American College of Prosthodontists answer key. Aggregate resident performance by postgraduate year and subject domain was obtained from the ACP database. Descriptive statistics summarized accuracy, and differences among LLMs were evaluated using Cochran Q, chi-squared, or Fisher exact tests (&#x3b1;=.05). **RESULTS:** Across both years, all LLMs outperformed residents in most content areas, including materials, esthetics, implant prosthodontics, pharmacology, and pathology. Residents performed better than all LLMs only in diagnosis (2024) and in infection control and microbiology (2025). Overall LLM performance for the 2024 and 2025 were statistically similar (P=.280 and P=.173, respectively). Gemini 2.5 Pro achieved the highest accuracy in 2024 (78.7%) and 2025 (85.3%). All LLMs scored higher on the 2025 examination compared with 2024, although year-to-year differences were statistically similar (overall, for Gemini 2.5 Pro P=.193, for ChatGPT-5 P=.314, for Claude Sonnet 4 P=.282). **CONCLUSIONS:** LLMs demonstrated high accuracy on consecutive national prosthodontic examinations and surpassed residents in most domains. Resident advantages in a few areas requiring contextual interpretation indicate domains in which resident accuracy exceeded model accuracy. LLMs show promise as supplemental educational tools, but responsible use requires AI literacy, verification skills, and assessment strategies that emphasize higher-order reasoning.
