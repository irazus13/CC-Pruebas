## Resumen

**BACKGROUND:** Glioblastoma (GBM) is the most common and aggressive primary brain tumor with a poor prognosis due to its immunosuppressive microenvironment. Reliable prognostic biomarkers are still lacking. This study aims to construct an immune-related gene signature to predict the prognosis of GBM patients and explore the molecular characteristics of different immune subtypes. **METHODS:** The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA), the Chinese Glioma Genome Atlas (CGGA), and Gene Expression Omnibus Series (GSE) 108474 datasets were integrated to classify cold and hot tumor subtypes via single-sample gene set enrichment analysis (ssGSEA) and partitioning around medoids (PAM) clustering. Prognostic genes were identified via weighted gene co-expression network analysis (WGCNA) and screened with 101 machine learning (ML) algorithms. A risk-scoring model was developed, and gene expression was validated via single-cell transcriptomics and immunohistochemistry (IHC). **RESULTS:** Seven core genes were identified, with area under the curve (AUC) values of 0.75-0.88 for distinguishing cold/hot subtypes. The model exhibited stable predictive performance in external validation cohorts (AUC =0.55-0.69). The survival rate of the high-risk cold tumor cohort was significantly lower than that of the low-risk hot tumor cohort. **CONCLUSIONS:** Our study identifies key genes and molecular mechanisms underlying the immune microenvironment of GBM. The prognostic model developed lays a theoretical foundation for personalized immunotherapy and stratified treatments.
