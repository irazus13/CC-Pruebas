## Resumen

Beta-arrestins (&#x3b2;arrs) are key regulators and transducers of G-protein coupled receptor signaling; however, little is known of how &#x3b2;arrs communicate with their downstream effectors. Here, we delineate structural mechanisms underlying &#x3b2;arr-mediated signal transduction. Using cryo-electron microscopy, we elucidate how &#x3b2;arr1 recruits and activates the non-receptor tyrosine kinase Src, a well-established signaling partner of &#x3b2;arrs. &#x3b2;arr1 engages Src SH3 through two distinct sites, each employing a different recognition mechanism: a polyproline motif in the N-domain and a non-proline-based interaction in the central crest region. At both sites &#x3b2;arr1 interacts with the aromatic surface of SH3, disrupting the autoinhibited conformation of Src and directly triggering its allosteric activation. This structural evidence establishes &#x3b2;arr1 as an active regulatory protein rather than a passive scaffold and suggests a potentially general mechanism for &#x3b2;arr-mediated signaling across diverse effectors.
