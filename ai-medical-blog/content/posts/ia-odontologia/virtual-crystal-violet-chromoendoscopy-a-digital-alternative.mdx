## Resumen

**BACKGROUND AND AIMS:** Routine use of crystal violet (CV) chromoendoscopy for pit pattern-based colorectal lesion characterization is limited by safety concerns. We developed and clinically validated an artificial intelligence (AI)-based virtual crystal violet (vCV) chromoendoscopy technique that reproduces CV-equivalent images without dye. **METHODS:** Using an unsupervised image-to-image translation framework (UVCGANv2), we trained a model to transform endoscopic images into vCV images. This retrospective study included 71 colorectal lesions with paired CV and narrow-band imaging (NBI) images. Six endoscopists independently evaluated randomized magnifying endoscopy images of vCV, CV, and NBI. **RESULTS:** Interobserver agreement for pit pattern interpretation with vCV was similar to conventional CV, with minimal differences. Quantitative image analysis demonstrated morphological fidelity between vCV and CV. Diagnostic accuracy of vCV for histological prediction was similar to CV and numerically higher than NBI. **CONCLUSIONS:** AI-based vCV chromoendoscopy can replicate the diagnostic interpretability of conventional CV staining without dye application.
