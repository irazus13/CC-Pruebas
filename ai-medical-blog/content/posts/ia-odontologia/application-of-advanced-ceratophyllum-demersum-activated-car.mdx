## Resumen

Water contamination by cationic dyes such as crystal violet (CV) is a significant environmental concern due to their toxicity and persistence. This study investigated and optimized CV adsorption onto Ceratophyllum demersum activated carbon (CDAC) using the Box-Behnken design (BBD) and artificial neural network (ANN) models. CDAC exhibited a specific surface area of 190.56&#x2009;m2&#x2009;g-1 and a mesoporous structure containing oxygen- and phosphorus-bearing functional groups. Batch adsorption experiments showed adsorption capacities ranging from 95 to 290&#x2009;mg&#x2009;g-1, with adsorption following pseudo-second-order kinetics and exhibiting favorable isotherm behavior. Although the ANN model produced lower prediction errors (&#x2264;&#x2009;8%) than the BBD model (&#x2265;&#x2009;20%), the BBD model was considered more reliable given the limited experimental dataset. Optimization predicted a maximum adsorption capacity of 258.76&#x2009;mg&#x2009;g-1 and a removal efficiency of 86% at an initial CV concentration of 150&#x2009;mg&#x2009;L-1, a CDAC dose of 0.5&#x2009;g&#x2009;L-1, and a contact time of 10&#x2009;min. Germination assays using Lens culinaris seeds indicated reduced phytotoxicity after treatment. These results demonstrate that CDAC is an effective and sustainable adsorbent for CV removal from aqueous solutions.
